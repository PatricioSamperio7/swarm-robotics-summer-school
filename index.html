About the Program

The Swarm Robotics Summer School is a rigorous technical intensive designed for practitioners seeking to master the complexities of distributed robotics systems. Unlike theoretical seminars, this program emphasizes engineering execution and real-world implementation.

Participants will dive deep into swarm intelligence, focusing on the decentralized coordination of multiple agents. The curriculum is built entirely on the ROS 2 framework, ensuring students acquire industry-standard skills in middleware communication, DDS protocols, and scalable robotics architectures.

Technical Depth

Focusing on ROS 2 Humble/Jazzy, distributed Linux environments, and hardware-in-the-loop testing. We bridge the gap between algorithmic abstraction and physical deployment.

Program Structure

Week 1

Foundations

  • Linux robotics environment optimization
  • ROS 2 architecture & Workspace management
  • DDS communication & Quality of Service (QoS)
  • Gazebo Ignition multi-robot simulation
  • Distributed systems communication theory
  • Robot networking & Latency management

Week 2

Swarm Intelligence

  • Consensus algorithms & Distributed voting
  • Formation control & Path planning
  • Behavior-based robotics (Subsumption)
  • Global/Local localization strategies
  • Multi-agent communication protocols
  • Decentralized task allocation

Week 3

Implementation

  • Hardware-software integration (Embedded)
  • Sensor fusion in multi-robot contexts
  • ROS nodes cross-deployment
  • Real-time robot communication protocols
  • Collaborative debugging techniques
  • Swarm testing in controlled environments

Final Stage

Demonstration

  • Distributed swarm deployment (Physical)
  • Technical defense & Peer review
  • System performance evaluation
  • Final Certification
