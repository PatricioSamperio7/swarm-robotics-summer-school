About the Program

The Swarm Robotics Summer School was established to address the growing complexity of decentralized systems in modern engineering. While theoretical research in multi-agent systems has flourished, a significant gap remains between algorithmic simulation and the physical execution of coordinated robot fleets. This program exists to bridge that divide, providing a dedicated space for intensive, practitioner-focused learning.

By focusing on the implementation of distributed intelligence, we foster a collaborative environment where innovation is driven by real-world constraints. Participants engage in a rigorous curriculum designed to transform abstract mathematical rules into resilient, collaborative robotic systems capable of navigating the uncertainties of physical environments.

Our mission is to prepare the next generation of robotics engineers to lead the deployment of swarm technologies across industrial and societal sectors. By mastering the coordination of autonomous agents, our participants are equipped to design systems that maximize collaborative efficiency and deliver meaningful real-world impact through distributed intelligence.

Certification & Recognition

Academic Validation

Participants who successfully complete the 25-hour technical program and the final swarm demonstration will receive an official IEEE participation certificate issued by the organizing Student Chapter.

Practitioner Training

This recognition validates the completion of advanced practitioner-level training in ROS 2 distributed architectures, DDS communication protocols, and hardware-in-the-loop deployment.

Project Portfolio

Beyond the certificate, participants leave with a portfolio-ready project, including a documented technical repository and a validated demonstration of collaborative robotics implementation.

Application Process

Eligibility & Selection

Selection based on technical profile and motivation:

  • Motivation statement quality.
  • Programming experience.
  • Academic interest in Swarm Robotics.
  • Limited capacity cohort (approx. 20–25 participants) to ensure hands-on mentoring.

Technical Recommendations

  • Ubuntu 22.04 LTS recommended: Provides optimal ROS 2 Humble or Jazzy compatibility for native development.
  • C++/Python: Foundational knowledge in OOP is recommended.
