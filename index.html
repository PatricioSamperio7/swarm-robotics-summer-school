Academic Overview

The 2026 Swarm Intelligence Summer School is a practitioner-oriented program focused on the engineering challenges of multi-robot coordination. Hosted by the IEEE RAS Student Branch at FIME, UANL.

Application Process

Target Participants

  • Senior undergraduate students
  • Graduate (MSc/PhD) students
  • Early-stage robotics researchers

Prerequisites

  • Programming experience preferred
  • Linux familiarity recommended
  • Basic robotics knowledge

Program Scope

  • Limited Cohort: 20–30 participants
  • Official IEEE Participation Certificate
  • Hardware-in-the-loop deployment

Expected Learning Outcomes

Design advanced ROS 2 distributed architectures for multi-agent systems.

Implement decentralized coordination algorithms and emergent behaviors.

Integrate embedded hardware with ROS 2 for physical swarm deployment.

Detailed Program

Week 1: ROS 2 & Distributed Communication

Mastering ROS 2 architecture, distributed communication layers, DDS, and Quality of Service (QoS) tuning. Focus on scalable simulation and multi-robot namespaces.

Week 2: Coordination & Hardware Deployment

Implementing cooperative control algorithms and decentralized decision-making. Deployment into physical embedded systems and final swarm validation.

Official Certification

Participants completing the program will receive an official participation certificate issued by the IEEE RAS Student Branch organizers.
