Swarm Robotics Summer School 2026

Design and Implementation of Distributed Robotics Swarms using ROS 2

FIME | UANL | MONTERREY

About the Summer School

The IEEE RAS Student Chapter at UANL presents an intensive 2-week hybrid program designed to bridge the gap between theoretical swarm intelligence and real-world implementation using the ROS 2 framework.

Course Focus

Advanced study of distributed systems, multi-robot communication protocols, and emergent behavior control using C++.

Hybrid Format

25 instructional hours. Week 1 focuses on theoretical foundations (Online), while Week 2 emphasizes physical deployment (On-site).

Technical Program

Week 1: Foundations

Swarm Theory & Complexity

Analysis of self-organization, rules of interaction, and complex emergent behaviors in biological and artificial systems.

Distributed Middleware

ROS 2 architecture for large-scale fleets, including discovery protocols, namespaces, and node lifecycle management.

Networking & DDS

Deep dive into Data Distribution Service (DDS), Quality of Service (QoS) profiles, and communication in unreliable networks.

Week 2: Implementation

Embedded Integration

Porting ROS 2 to microcontrollers (micro-ROS) and sensor fusion for collective perception in the swarm.

Cooperative Algorithms

Implementation of formation control, consensus algorithms, and decentralized decision-making logic.

Final Deployment

Deployment of a complete swarm system to solve a real-world industrial or exploration challenge.

Application

Target Profile

Designed for senior undergraduate students, graduate researchers, and R&D engineers interested in multi-robot systems.

Requirements

Proficiency in C++ or Python and a working knowledge of Linux environments are necessary for the implementation phase.
